Digital Citizenship Reflection

Being a responsible digital citizen means they think before clicking, posting, or sharing online. They protect their personal information by using strong and personal passwords, adjusting privacy settings, and avoiding suspicious links or fake websites to avoid viruses and hackers. They show respect and kindness in online interactions by avoiding cyberbullying, hate speech, curse words, and harmful comments. Responsible digital citizens also practice digital ethics by respecting copyright, giving credit to sources, and not spreading misinformation or fake news. Overall, being a responsible digital citizen means using technology in a way that is safe, respectful, and beneficial to oneself and the online community. We practice online safety by protecting our personal information, respecting others online, and thinking critically before sharing content. "Digital Citizenship is not only about using technology, but about using it responsibly, ethically, and respectfully"
